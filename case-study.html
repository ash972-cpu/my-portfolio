The Core Problem

Unplanned patient readmissions place a massive strain on healthcare facilities. When a patient returns to the hospital within 30 days of discharge, it indicates potential gaps in initial treatment, premature release, or a lack of outpatient monitoring. My objective was to construct a predictive classification model using historical healthcare data to catch these high-risk patients *before* they are sent home.

Data Architecture & Preprocessing

1. Handling the Class Imbalance

In standard medical datasets, positive readmissions are heavily outweighed by standard successful discharges. Training a raw model on this results in a biased classifier that simply guesses "No Readmission" to achieve high baseline accuracy. I countered this by applying class weighting techniques and synthetic over-sampling to balance the vector space.

2. Outlier Normalization

Medical records frequently contain erroneous biological inputs or massive variations in lab stay durations. Using standard deviation filters, I isolated and treated extreme numerical anomalies to ensure the gradient descent converged smoothly.

The Pipeline in Practice

The model was tested across several Scikit-Learn algorithms, shifting from basic Logistic Regressions up to complex ensemble decision trees. The final output generates a probability score attached to the patient's ID, allowing hospital discharge coordinators to look at a sorted dashboard and offer targeted outpatient support to the top 15% most vulnerable patients.
